Chapter 4: Environmental Pollution

Environmental pollution is the contamination of natural surroundings that causes harm to living organisms.

Types of Pollution:
1. Air Pollution – Caused by vehicles, industries, and burning of fossil fuels.
2. Water Pollution – Caused by industrial waste, sewage discharge, and oil spills.
3. Soil Pollution – Caused by use of pesticides, fertilizers, and waste dumping.
4. Noise Pollution – Caused by traffic, construction, and industrial activities.

Effects:
- Global warming and climate change
- Loss of biodiversity
- Respiratory diseases in humans
- Soil infertility

Control Measures:
- Use of renewable energy
- Waste management and recycling
- Planting trees
- Government policies and awareness programs
